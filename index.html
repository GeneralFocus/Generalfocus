06 — Principles

How I think about
building systems.

01
Reliability over hype

New tools earn their place by solving real problems, not by being new. Every architecture decision is evaluated against proven reliability patterns before chasing the latest trend. Infrastructure that works at 2am matters more than infrastructure that demos well.

02
Automation over manual processes

If you do something twice, automate it. Manual processes are failure points with memory leaks. Infrastructure that can't deploy itself can't scale and teams that rely on toil will always be firefighting instead of building.

03
Clear architecture over complexity

The best infrastructure design is the one the next engineer can reason about during an incident. Cleverness is a liability. Clarity is an asset. If I can't explain a system in a diagram in under five minutes, it's probably too complex.

04
Documentation over assumptions

Undocumented architecture exists only in the heads of the people who built it — and they leave. Runbooks, ADRs and READMEs are first-class engineering deliverables. Writing is part of the job, not a nice-to-have after.
